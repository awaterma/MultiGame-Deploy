Basics


Action takes place on the board, which symbolizes the Tablon watershed. Players arrive at the watershed as it was in its virgin state and work to develop the territory over time. Development takes place with the replacing of "green" tokens with colored, lettered tokens each player possesses. These tokens symbolize different types of development in the territory (ranching, agriculture, and tree nurseries, for example). Each development type has an economic "reward" or incentive. We have articulated these rewards as points. So each type of token gives you a certain number of points, as well as modifying the territory. Some tokens cause little ecological changes (our "F" or managed forest token), others cause change and damage. For example, the "M" or Moderate Ranching and the "I" or Intensive Ranching tokens deforest the territory. We have placed environmental restrictions regarding deforestation and development in riparian zones which are detailed below.


Movement


Tokens are dragged from the left onto the game board in the center of the screen, when the player has his/her turn. Players can only drag tokens onto cells (territories) that they own. Ownership is demarked by the background color of each cell, with each player having a corresponding color to that beneath each territory. You can determine each player's color by looking at the player list on the right side of the screen and looking at the color icon associated with each registered player's name.


During puzzle mode, players can suggest other players move tokens they own to different locations on the board. Players may only make 1 suggestion per player during their own turn. Suggestions are made by dragging other users tokens to the desired location. Note: dragging other player's tokens is subject to the same set of restrictions as your own tokens (Intensive's cannot be adjacent to one another, all tokens must be within a user's territory or on the riparian trees forming the boundary).


How to Win


Sierra Springs (Manantiales) can be "won" when played in competitive modalities (Classic and Silvopastoral) and can only progress in puzzle modalities when all players have "won" (or achieved territorial development equivalent to that competitive modalities winning score). What this means in practice, is that the first player to score 24 points in the Classic game, wins that mode. The first player to score 32 points in Silvopastoral, wins that mode. The "Basic Puzzle" can only be resolved when all 4 players have scored 24 points. "Advanced Puzzle" can only be resolved when all 4 players have 32 points.


The Game


Sierra Springs (Manantiales) consists of a board divided into four territories bordered by four creeks.


Each of the four players have the task of colonizing one territory. The entire locale consists of forty-eight sites. Each site represents one Hectare (or any other unit of measurement, for example, acres, square miles, etc). One large blue token represents the main spring. It is located in the center of the board. Forty-eight green tokens represent virgin forest or secondary re-growth during play. A simple table that counts olive tokens removed from the board during development.


Each player colonizing a quadrant has thirty tokens, divided into lots of six, representing five types of land use. Each type of use has a distinct value (F equates to Forest Management and is valued at one; M equals Moderate Grazing and is valued at two; I equates to Intensive grazing and is valued at three, E equates to Grassland Exclusion for Silvopastoral System Establishment and has no value; S equates to a productive silvopastoral system and is valued at four).


In the competitive game, a random number from 1-6 is displayed and chosen by the player on each turn. If it is a "6," the player has experienced a "bad year" and loses his/her turn. In the puzzle game, no such device is used. Players are allowed to make any legal move once it is their turn.


A player colonizes a chosen site with a token that represents one possible type of development. Some tokens may be placed freely in any location, while others may not. Each different type of development is valued distinctly. Points awarded per type of colonization, allow residents to acquire "material benefits" These benefits are available to the player when he/she attains a certain number of points, and are represented by a specific resource card. In the competitive mode of the game, whoever meets a specified number of points first, wins the game. In the puzzle mode, all players must meet the winning score, otherwise, all lose the game.


Most types of development involve site deforestation. If the total deforestation exceeds a certain threshold, the spring that supplies water to the players dries up, all residents must migrate and all players lose the game.


The same occurs if more than two sites surrounding the main spring are deforested. in the case that more than two sites on a creek are deforested, that creek temporarily runs out of water and the neighboring players lose all development in that locale, except for sites that are "managed forest." These lost sites may be recolonized on the subsequent turn by whoever chooses to do so first.


When a player creates any of the above three situations, the next player must avoid "check-mate" by reforesting the proper site and resolving the condition; this involves the subtraction of points from a player’s current score.


Modalities


How many ways can this game be played?


The game has two land development versions: (A) Land development with conventional land use: managed forest, moderate ranching with moderate grazing, ranching with intense (over)grazing. And (B) Land development with conventional land use and a silvo-pastoral innovation. These two versions can be played in two social modalities:


  1. Competition: the first player that reaches a certain number of points wins (24/32pts).
  2. Cooperation: collective planning for equity. Everyone works to solve the game as a puzzle which can only be resolved when all players have the number of points required to win. (24/32pts).


What this means is that the game can spin through 4 different sections, starting in the following order:

  1. "Classic" competitive mode
  2. "Basic Puzzle" puzzle mode
  3. "Silvopastoral" competitive mode
  4. "Advanced PUzzle" puzzle mode

Players may initiate games at any of the above modalities while configuring new games.
